I'm an MSc student at [ETH Zürich](https://ethz.ch) specializing in Visual & Interactive Computing with a minor in Machine Learning. I'm currently wrapping up my master's thesis as a research intern at NVIDIA in Helsinki, working on the error--correlation tradeoff in [ReSTIR](https://research.nvidia.com/publication/2020-07_spatiotemporal-reservoir-resampling-real-time-ray-tracing-dynamic-direct) — a family of real-time Monte Carlo rendering algorithms that reuse path samples across pixels and frames.

My research interest sits at the intersection of physically-based rendering and real-time GPU algorithms. My thesis work introduced a geometry-based pixel compatibility heuristic for guiding spatial neighbor selection in ReSTIR, published as [Compatibility-Guided Neighbor Selection for ReSTIR](/projects/1_project/) at HPG 2026.

Before ETH, I studied computer science at Oregon State University Cascades. There I published work on [river flow forecasting](/projects/2_project/) using stacked GRU ensembles and built [LevelSage](https://github.com/orion-junkins/river-level-forecasting), a production system delivering real-time forecasts for 15 rivers on AWS at under $0.25 per model per month.
